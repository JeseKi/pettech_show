## Target Feel

Write like a科技公众号编辑, not like a release-note summarizer.

- Open with lead paragraphs that make a concrete claim or judgment. The title lives in `metadata.json`, not the Markdown body.
- The first 2 to 3 paragraphs should tell readers why today's updates matter before diving into details.
- Pick 2 to 4 projects that best support the main thesis. Do not give every source equal weight.
- Translate product updates into reader impact: workflow change, competitive signal, adoption implication, or industry trend.
- Keep enough detail that readers can tell what actually shipped or changed.
- Close with one compact takeaway, not a generic summary.

## Structural Pattern

Use this rhythm when it fits the material:

1. Lead with a hook and thesis (no top-level title in Markdown).
2. Section 1: the most representative update.
3. Section 2 and 3: supporting cases with interpretation.
4. Synthesis: what these updates say about the market.
5. Closing line with a strong takeaway.

## Tone

- Prefer confident, crisp judgment over bland balance.
- Sound informed and editorial, but stay fact-grounded.
- Keep paragraphs readable and varied. Some short emphatic paragraphs are good.
- Bullets are allowed only when they clearly improve clarity inside a section.
- Specificity matters more than volume. Each section should carry concrete facts, implications, and reader-facing judgment.
- Be concise. Cut repeated framing and generic explanation.
- Main drafts are text-only. Do not preserve, embed, link, or placeholder source images in `main.md`.
- When a source image adds evidence, context, operation steps, or conversion value, translate the useful information into prose instead of carrying the image into the draft.

## Avoid

- "下面我们来看"
- "今天整理了几个项目更新"
- Treating the article like a changelog dump
- Listing every feature with equal importance
- Writing only abstract trend commentary without concrete update details
- Referring to local files, prompts, directories, or the writing process
